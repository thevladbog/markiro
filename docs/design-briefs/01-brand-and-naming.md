# Design Brief 01 — Brand & Naming

> First stage. The name and identity produced here feed every later brief:
> the design system, both UIs and the landing page all ship in final branding.

## 1. Naming

### Why the current name must go

The working name **Bottle [CODE]** is tied to bottles. The platform is
universal across Chestny ZNAK product groups (beer, water, soft drinks today;
dairy, supplements, apparel possible tomorrow). The name must not anchor us to
one container type or product category.

### Naming criteria

A candidate name must:

1. Not reference a specific container/product type (no bottle, can, keg…).
2. Work in both Latin and Cyrillic contexts (spoken and written); ideally one
   spelling that Russian customers can pronounce over the phone without
   spelling it out — sales to small plants happen by phone.
3. Have an available domain: `.ru` plus `.com` or `.io`.
4. Be clear of conflicting Russian trademarks (preliminary check is enough at
   this stage; legal check follows).
5. Hint at the domain — marking, codes, traceability, production line — without
   being generic ("MarkSystem") or bureaucratic.

### Directions to explore (examples, not finals)

- **Code / mark:** MarkFlow, Kodly, Метка.Про
- **Line / flow:** LineTrack, Konveer
- **Trace / transparency:** TraceOne, SledOK

### Naming deliverable

5–7 candidates, each with: rationale, domain availability, quick trademark
scan result, how it sounds in a Russian phone conversation. We pick together;
expect 1–2 iterations.

## 2. Visual identity

### Deliverables

- **Logo:** primary, monochrome, and a compact mark usable as favicon,
  app icon and a tiny header logo inside both apps. Must survive one-color
  print (documents, label headers).
- **Color palette:**
  - Brand colors for office mode (admin, landing).
  - **Semantic status colors** — green (OK), red (error), amber (duplicate /
    attention), blue (syncing) — designed as first-class brand citizens, not
    an afterthought: on the shop floor they _are_ the interface. Must pass
    contrast in light and dark themes and remain distinguishable for
    color-blind users when paired with icons/shapes.
- **Typography:** one family (or a pair) with full **Cyrillic support**,
  excellent legibility at large sizes (floor mode: 18px minimum body, huge
  numerals for counters) and comfortable density at small sizes (admin
  tables). Numerals matter: counters, quantities, codes — tabular figures
  required.
- **Iconography direction:** stroke style, corner radius, grid — the design
  system (brief 02) will extend it.
- **Mini brand guideline:** 5–10 pages covering the above plus do/don't.

### Tone of voice

We talk to production people, not IT people. Plain, businesslike, concrete.
No "digital transformation of marking business processes" — instead: "the
line keeps running, no fines, reports in two minutes". Russian first; English
mirrors the same register. Error messages and UI copy follow this tone
(briefs 03–04).

### Feel

Trustworthy industrial-grade tool with modern SaaS polish. Not playful/consumer,
not corporate-gray. A small plant owner should feel "this is serious software
I can afford"; an operator should feel "this thing is on my side".

## Optional add-on

A short **audio signal set** for the line station (success / error / duplicate /
box complete) — distinct tones recognizable in a noisy workshop. If sound
design is out of scope, we will source library sounds ourselves; flag your
preference.
